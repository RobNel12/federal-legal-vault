# I.R.C. § 6662 – Accuracy-Related Penalties

**Text:** (a) Imposition of penalty. – If any portion of an underpayment of tax required to be shown on a return is attributable to — (1) negligence or disregard of rules or regulations, (2) any substantial understatement of income tax, (3) any substantial valuation misstatement under chapter 1, (4) any substantial overstatement of pension liabilities, (5) any substantial estate or gift tax valuation understatement, or (6) any claimed tax benefit from a transaction lacking economic substance or failing to meet any similar federal tax law requirement, there shall be added to the tax an amount equal to 20 percent of the amount of such underpayment.

**Key Points:**
- 20% penalty on underpayment due to negligence, substantial understatement (>10% of tax due or $5,000), etc.
- Can be increased to 40% for gross valuation misstatements or undisclosed foreign financial asset understatements.
- Includes "economic substance" doctrine and undisclosed transactions.
