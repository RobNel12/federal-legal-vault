# I.R.C. § 162 – Trade or Business Expenses

**Text:** There shall be allowed as a deduction all the ordinary and necessary expenses paid or incurred during the taxable year in carrying on any trade or business, including:
(1) a reasonable allowance for salaries or other compensation for personal services actually rendered;
(2) traveling expenses (including amounts expended for meals and lodging);
(3) rentals or other payments required to be made for the continued use or possession, for purposes of the trade or business, of any property;
(4) the amount of any business tax (except federal income, war‑profits, and excess‑profits taxes) paid or accrued within the taxable year; and
(5) losses, including losses from sales or exchanges of property.

**Key Points:**
- “Ordinary and necessary” test from *Welch v. Helvering*, 290 U.S. 111 (1933).
- Must be incurred in connection with a trade or business (not personal, investment, or capital).
- Limitations: §§ 262 (personal expenses), 263 (capital expenditures), 274 (entertainment, etc.), 280A (home office), 280F (listed property).

**Landmark Cases:**
- *Welch v. Helvering*, 290 U.S. 111 (1933) – “ordinary and necessary” defined.
- *Commissioner v. Lincoln Savings & Loan Ass’n*, 403 U.S. 345 (1971) – points to consider reasonableness.
- *Freeman v. Commissioner*, 66 T.C. 364 (1976) (aff’d, 591 F.2d 753 (9th Cir. 1979)) – expense must be “appropriate and helpful” to the business.
- *INDOPCO, Inc. v. Commissioner*, 503 U.S. 79 (1992) – capitalization of facilitative expenses.
