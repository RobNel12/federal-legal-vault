# I.R.C. § 63 – Taxable Income

**Text:** (a) General Rule. – Except as provided in subsection (b), taxable income is gross income minus the deductions allowed by this chapter (other than the standard deduction).
(b) Individuals who do not itemize deductions. – For any individual who does not itemize deductions under section 63, taxable income is adjusted gross income minus the standard deduction allowed under section 63(c).

**Key Points:**
- Taxable income = AGI − (itemized deductions OR standard deduction) − (personal exemption deduction, prior to TCJA).
- Forms the base to which tax rates are applied (§ 1).
- After TCJA, personal exemption deduction is zero (2018‑2025); standard deduction increased.

**Components:**
- Gross income (§ 61)
- Above‑the‑line deductions (to arrive at AGI, § 62)
- Below‑the‑line deductions: either itemized (Schedule A) or standard deduction (§ 63(c))
- Personal exemption (pre‑TCJA) (§ 151)

**Landmark Cases:**
- *Helvering v. Independent Life Ins. Co.*, 292 U.S. 371 (1934) – definition of “taxable income” for corporations.
- *United States v. Burke*, 504 U.S. 229 (1992) – damages for back pay are taxable as wages, not as compensation for personal injury (relevant to what is included in gross income before deductions).
