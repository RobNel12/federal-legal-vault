# I.R.C. § 212 – Expenses for Production of Income

**Text:** In the case of an individual, there shall be allowed as a deduction all the ordinary and necessary expenses paid or incurred during the taxable year:
(1) for the production or collection of income;
(2) for the management, conservation, or maintenance of property held for the production of income; or
(3) in connection with the determination, collection, or refund of any tax.

**Key Points:**
- Similar “ordinary and necessary” test as § 162, but applies to investment and other non‑business activities.
- Subject to 2% AGI floor for individuals (pre‑TCJA) – now suspended for tax years 2018‑2025 by TCJA § 11045 (but may return after 2025).
- Includes investment advice, custodial fees, tax preparation fees, etc.

**Landmark Cases:**
- *Commissioner v. Groetzinger*, 480 U.S. 23 (1987) – activity must be regular and continuous to be a trade or business; otherwise may fall under § 212.
- *Woodward v. Commissioner*, 397 U.S. 572 (1970) – expenses to produce taxable income are deductible under § 212.
- *Henry v. Commissioner*, 36 T.C. 879 (1961) – legal expenses related to tax controversies.

**Regulations:**
