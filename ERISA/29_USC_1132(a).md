# 29 U.S.C. § 1132(a) - Civil Enforcement: Persons Empowered to Bring Civil Action

A civil action may be brought—
(1) by a participant or beneficiary—
(A) for the relief provided for in subsection (c), or
(B) to recover benefits due to him under the terms of his plan, to enforce his rights under the terms of the plan, or to clarify his rights to future benefits under the terms of the plan;
(2) by the Secretary, or by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title;
(3) by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary (A) to enjoin any act or practice which violates any provision of this subchapter or the terms of the plan, or (B) to obtain other appropriate equitable relief (i) to redress such violations or (ii) to enforce any provisions of this subchapter or the terms of the plan;
(4) by the Secretary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title;
(5) by the Secretary, or by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title in the case of a breach of fiduciary duty;
(6) by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title in the case of a violation of section 1106 of this title;
(7) by the Secretary, or by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title in the case of a violation of section 1107 of this title;
(8) by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title in the case of a violation of section 1108 of this title;
(9) by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title in the case of a violation of section 1104(a) of this title;
(10) by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title in the case of a violation of section 1105 of this title.

## Overview
Section 1132(a) is the primary enforcement mechanism of ERISA, specifying who may bring civil actions and for what relief. It creates a comprehensive enforcement scheme for ERISA rights.

## Key Provisions
- **1132(a)(1)(A)**: Participant/beneficiary actions for relief under 1132(c) (penalties for failure to provide information)
- **1132(a)(1)(B)**: Participant/beneficiary actions to recover benefits, enforce rights, clarify future benefits
- **1132(a)(2)**: Secretary/participant/beneficiary/fiduciary for relief under 1109 (breach of fiduciary duty)
- **1132(a)(3)**: Participant/beneficiary/fiduciary for injunctive/equitable relief
- **1132(a)(4)-(10)**: Additional enforcement provisions for specific violations

## Applications
- **Benefit claims**: Recovery of denied benefits under 1132(a)(1)(B)
- **Fiduciary breach**: Actions under 1132(a)(2) and (a)(3)
- **Injunctive relief**: Stopping ongoing violations under 1132(a)(3)
- **ERISA enforcement**: Comprehensive remedial scheme
