# ERISA § 502(a) - Civil Enforcement

**29 U.S.C. § 1132(a)**

A civil action may be brought—

(1) by a participant or beneficiary—
(A) for the relief provided for in subsection (c) of this section, or
(B) to recover benefits due to him under the terms of his plan, to enforce his rights under the terms of the plan, or to clarify his rights to future benefits under the terms of the plan;
(2) by the Secretary, or by a participant, beneficiary or fiduciary for appropriate relief under section 1109 of this title;
(3) by a participant, beneficiary, or fiduciary (A) to enjoin any act or practice which violates any provision of this subchapter or the terms of the plan, or (B) to obtain other appropriate equitable relief (i) to redress such violations or (ii) to enforce any provisions of this subchapter or the terms of the plan;
(4) by the Secretary, or by a participant, or beneficiary for appropriate relief under section 1109(c) of this title;
(5) by a participant or beneficiary to—
(A) review any matter related to such plan which is the subject of a hearing under section 1133 of this title, providing the orderly functioning of the plans involved;
(6) by the Secretary; and
(7) by a participant or beneficiary who is adversely affected by any act or practice which violates any provision of this subchapter, to obtain (or to recover) any benefit plan documents, any plan description, any contract or other instrument under which the plan is established or operated.

## Key Subsections for Reimbursement Claims
- **§ 1132(a)(1)(B)**: To recover benefits due under the plan terms (often used for reimbursement claims based on plan language)
- **§ 1132(a)(3)**: To obtain "other appropriate equitable relief" to enforce plan provisions or redress violations (the basis for equitable lien claims like in Sereboff)

## Notes
- Section 502(a)(3) limits remedies to equitable relief (not legal damages for money)
- The Supreme Court has interpreted this narrowly in cases like Great-West and Sereboff
- Plan language must create a valid lien or equitable right to reimbursement
